# Chennai Transit Intelligence System (CTIS)

## Product Requirements Document (PRD)

**Version:** 1.0

**Project Type:** Progressive Web Application (PWA)

**Primary Domain:** Transportation Optimization & Graph Algorithms

**Development Status:** Planning Phase

---

## 1. Vision

The Chennai Transit Intelligence System (CTIS) aims to develop a unified multimodal transit planning platform that integrates the Chennai Metro, MRTS, and Suburban Railway networks into a single intelligent application.

The platform will enable commuters to identify optimal routes based on travel time, fare, transfers, and waiting time while also providing predictive scheduling for Chennai Metro Phase II corridors.

Beyond route planning, CTIS will serve as an educational and analytical platform demonstrating the application of graph algorithms, optimization techniques, geospatial visualization, and predictive analytics in solving real-world transportation problems.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

Chennai's rail-based public transportation system consists of multiple independent transit networks, including the Chennai Metro, MRTS, and Suburban Railway. Although these systems collectively provide extensive city-wide connectivity, passengers often need to switch between different transport modes without access to a single platform that optimizes the entire journey.

Existing route planners primarily focus on finding the shortest path but often overlook practical commuter concerns such as waiting time, transfer convenience, multimodal connectivity, and future transit expansion.

Additionally, there is currently no publicly available platform that models the proposed Chennai Metro Phase II network alongside existing services to estimate future travel times, schedules, and connectivity.

The Chennai Transit Intelligence System aims to address these challenges by integrating all major rail transit networks into a unified graph-based model capable of intelligent route optimization, simulation, and predictive analysis.

---

## 3. Objectives

### Primary Objectives

- Develop a unified transit planning platform integrating Chennai Metro, MRTS, and Suburban Railway networks.
- Model the complete transportation network as a weighted graph.
- Compute optimal routes based on:
  - Minimum travel time
  - Minimum fare
  - Minimum number of transfers
  - Minimum expected waiting time
- Provide an intuitive and multilingual user interface as a Progressive Web Application (PWA).

### Secondary Objectives

- Integrate the proposed Chennai Metro Phase II corridors into the network.
- Predict schedules and train frequencies for future metro corridors using data derived from existing operational lines.
- Visualize the transportation network using interactive maps.
- Simulate network disruptions such as station closures and train delays.
- Provide network analytics demonstrating graph properties and algorithm performance.

### Educational Objectives

- Demonstrate the practical application of graph algorithms in transportation systems.
- Showcase software engineering practices including documentation, version control, modular architecture, and testing.
- Build a portfolio-quality project suitable for technical interviews and software engineering roles.

---

## 4. Target Users

The Chennai Transit Intelligence System is designed for the following user groups:

### Daily Commuters
Passengers who regularly travel using Chennai Metro, MRTS, and Suburban Railway and require optimized route recommendations.

### Students
Students commuting to schools and colleges across Chennai who benefit from cost-effective and time-efficient travel planning.

### Tourists and Occasional Travelers
Users unfamiliar with Chennai's public transport system who require a simple and integrated journey planner.

### Researchers and Urban Planners
Individuals interested in studying transportation networks, route optimization, passenger movement, and infrastructure planning.

### Software Engineering and Data Structures Learners
Students and developers who wish to understand the practical application of graph algorithms, shortest path algorithms, and transportation analytics through an interactive platform.
